Order
Do not confuse order with molecularity!
Order describes the dependence of rate on concentration. Molecularity is the number of molecules participating in the reaction.
$$(-r) = k[A]^{a}[B]^{b}[C]^{c}...$$ The overall order of the above rate equation is $a + b + c ...$ and the order with respect to $A, B, C$ ... is $a, b, c$ ... respectively.
In the examples given below, the overall order is used to classify a reaction as first order, second order, etc.

  1. Gas phase decomposition of ammonia on molybdenum $$\ce{2NH_3 ->[Mo] N_2 + 3H_2}$$
  2. Decomposition of nitrous oxide in presence of a hot platinum surface $$\ce{N_2O ->[Pt][~800K] N_2 + 1/2 O_2}$$
  3. Iodation of acetone in presence of $\ce{H+}$ $$\ce{CH_3COCH_3 + I_2_{(aq)} ->[H+] CH_3COCH_2I + HI_{(aq)}}$$

  1. Hydrolysis of butyl chloride $$C_4H_9Cl + H_2O \rightarrow C_4H_9OH + HCl$$ $$r = k[C_4H_9Cl]$$
  2. Dehydrogenation of ethane $$C_2H_6 \rightarrow C_2H_4 + H_2$$ $$r = -k[C_2H_6]$$
  3. Decomposition of nitrous chlorides $$\phi N=NCl \rightarrow \phi Cl + N_2$$ $$r = -k[\phi N=NCl]$$
  4. Acid hydrolysis of ethylene oxide to ethanediol $$H_2C(O)CH_2 + H_2O \rightarrow [H_2SO_4] HOCH_2CH_2OH$$ $$r_{H_2C(O)CH_2} = -k[H_2C(O)CH_2]$$
  5. Photolysis of acetone $$CH_3COCH_3 \rightarrow CH_2CO + CH_4$$ $$r = -k[CH_3COCH_3]$$
  6. Isomerization of n-butane $$\ce{nC_4 H_10 <=> iC_4H_10}$$ $$r = -k[nC_4 - iC_4/K_C]$$
  7. Alkaline hydrolysis of t-butyl bromide $$(CH_3)_3CBr_{(aq)} + OH^{-}_{(aq)} \rightarrow (CH_3)_3COH_{(aq)} + Br^{-}_{(aq)}$$ $$r = -k[(CH_3)_3CBr]$$
  8. Thermal decomposition of dinitrogen pentoxide $${2N_2O_5}_{(g)} \rightarrow {4NO_2}_{(g)} + {O_2}_{(g)}$$ $$r = -k[N_2O_5]$$
  9. Rearrangement of vinyl allyl ether to allyl acetaldehyde $$CH_2=CH-O-CH_2-CH=CH_2 \rightarrow CH_2=CH-CH_2-CH_2-CHO$$

  1. Gas phase reaction between hydrogen and iodine $${H_2}_{(g)} + {I_2}_{(g)} \rightarrow 2HI_{(g)}$$ $$r = -k[H_2][I_2]$$
  2. Gas phase decomposition of Hydrogen Iodide $$2HI_{(g)} \rightarrow {H_2}_{(g)} + {I_2}_{(g)}$$ $$r_{HI} = -k[HI]^{2}$$
  3. Reaction of bromide cyanide with methylamine$$CNBr + CH_3NH_2 \rightarrow CH_3Br + NCNH_2$$ $$r_{CNBr} = -k[CNBr][CH_3NH_2]$$
  4. Amination of o-nitrochlorobenze $$o-NO_2C_6H_4Cl + 2NH_3 \rightarrow o-NH_2C_6H_4NH_2 + NH_4Cl$$ $$r_{ONCB} = -k[ONCB][NH_3]$$
  5. Ethyl acetate and butanol react to produce butyl acetate and ethanol $$\ce{\underset{A}{CH_3COOC_2H_5} + \underset{B}{C_4H_9OH} <=> \underset{C}{CH_3COOC_4H_9} + \underset{D}{C_2H_5OH}}$$ $$r_A = -k[C_AC_B-C_CC_D/K_C]$$
  6. Reaction of peroxydisulfate ion with iodide ion in aqueous solution $${S_2O_8^{2-}}_{(aq)} + 3I^{-}_{(aq)} \rightarrow {2SO_4^{2-}}_{(aq)} + {I_3^{-}}_{(aq)}$$ $$r = -k[S_2O_8^{2-}][I^{-}]$$
  7. Disproportionation reaction of hypochlorite ion $$3ClO^{-}_{(aq)} \rightarrow {ClO_3^{-}}_{(aq)} + 2Cl^{-}_{(aq)}$$ $$r = -k[ClO^{-}]^2$$
  8. Reaction between formate ion and permanganate ion in aqueous solution $${3HCOO^{-}}_{(aq)} + {2MnO_4^{-}}_{(aq)} + {2H^{+}}_{(aq)} \rightarrow {2MnO_2}_{(s)} + {3HCO_3^{-}}_{(aq)} + {H_2O}_{(l)}$$ $$r = -k[MnO_4^{-}][HCOO^{-}]$$
  9. Reaction between hypochlorite ion and bromide ion $${ClO^{-}}_{(aq)} + {Br^{-}}_{(aq)} \rightarrow {BrO^-}_{(aq)} + {Cl^-}_{(aq)}$$ $$r = -k[ClO^-][Br^-]$$
  10. Synthesis of urea $$NH_4^+ + CNO^- \rightarrow {(NH_2)}_2CO$$ $$r = -k[NH_4^+][CNO^-]$$

  1. Formation of phosgene $$CO + Cl_2 \rightarrow COCl_2$$ $$r_{CO} = -k[Cl_2]^{1.5}[CO]$$
  2. Gas phase thermal decomposition of ethanal $$CH_3CHO \rightarrow CH_4 + CO$$ $$r_{CH_3CHO} = -k[CH_3CHO]^{\frac{3}{2}}$$
  3. Chlorine-catalysed decomposition of ozone $$2O_3 \rightarrow 3O_2$$ $$r = -k[O_3]^{\frac{3}{2}}$$

  1. Reaction of nitric oxide with chlorine (overall order of reaction is $3$) $$2NO + Cl_2 \rightarrow 2NOCl$$ $$r = -k[NO]^2[Cl_2]$$
  2. Reaction of nitric oxide with oxygen (overall order of reaction is $3$) $$2NO + O_2 \rightarrow 2NO_2$$ $$r = -k[NO]^2[Cl_2]$$
  3. Reaction between bromate ion and bromide ion in acidic medium (overall order of reaction is $4$)$${BrO_3^{-}}_{(aq)} + 5Br^{-}_{(aq)} + 6H^{+}_{(aq)} \rightarrow {3Br_2}_{(aq)} + {3H_2O}_{(l)}$$ $$r = -k[BrO_3^{-}][Br^{-}][H^{+}]^2$$
